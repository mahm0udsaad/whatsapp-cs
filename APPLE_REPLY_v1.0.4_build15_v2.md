Subject: Build 1.0.4 (15) — removed self-serve purchase path (addresses 3.1.1 / 3.1.3(c))

Hi,

Thank you for the detailed feedback on 3.1.1 and 3.1.3(c). We understand the concern: the service must not be available for an individual or consumer to purchase for themselves. We have changed the product to address this directly.

What we changed

1. Removed self-serve sign-up entirely. The website no longer lets anyone — individual or business — create an account or obtain the service on their own. The previous /signup form (which created an account instantly) has been replaced with a "request access" form that creates no account and no credentials. It only submits a business lead to our sales team.

2. Provisioning is now offline and business-only. An account is created and activated by our team only after we verify the requesting business and sign a commercial agreement with the organization. A consumer cannot self-purchase or self-activate the service through any path.

3. The iOS app itself sells nothing. The app has no sign-up screen, no pricing, no subscription UI, and no in-app purchase code of any kind. It is a companion client used by staff of an already-provisioned business account to sign in (employee login) and to link to that business's existing workspace on our platform via a one-time pairing code. There is no digital content or service for sale inside the app.

How this maps to your guidance

You offered two resolutions: (a) provide the service only to organizations, or (b) add In-App Purchase. We have taken path (a) — the service is now provisioned exclusively to organizations through an offline, verified business process, with no consumer self-purchase available anywhere in the app or on the website.
